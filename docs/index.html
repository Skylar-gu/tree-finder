      🌳 Before you climb…

      Terms of Use & Assumption of Risk (demonstration document — not legal advice)

      1. What this service is. Climbable-Trees ranks publicly-inventoried trees by how plausibly climbable they are, using species characteristics, trunk measurements, and open map data. It is a discovery tool.

      2. What this service is not. It is not a safety inspection, engineering assessment, or certification of any tree. No output — score, confidence, match category, or measurement — states that a tree, branch, or activity is safe.

      3. Estimates have errors. Every number is an estimate with an error band. Branch data is usually a guess from the species' typical shape, not a measurement. Trees change: they grow, rot, crack, and shed limbs without notice.

      4. Assumption of risk. Climbing trees can cause serious injury or death. If you climb, you do so entirely at your own risk, and you are responsible for inspecting the tree yourself and for your own decisions.

      5. Follow the law and the land. You are responsible for complying with local rules, park regulations, and property rights. A tree appearing here does not grant permission to climb it.

      6. Data & licenses. Tree records come from municipal open-data portals; map data © OpenStreetMap contributors; street photos © their Mapillary contributors (CC BY-SA 4.0).

      7. No liability. To the maximum extent permitted by law, the makers of this demonstration accept no liability for any harm arising from its use.

      ⚠️ This tool filters and ranks — it never certifies that a tree is safe to climb.
